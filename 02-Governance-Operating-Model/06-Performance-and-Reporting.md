# Purpose

This document establishes the approach used by Megastar Mortgage to measure, report, review, and continually improve the effectiveness of AI governance.

It defines:

- governance performance objectives;
- governance reporting principles;
- governance reporting audiences;
- governance performance indicators;
- management review expectations; and
- continual improvement activities.

This document measures the effectiveness of the governance program itself. It does not measure AI model performance, operational productivity, or technical system performance.

---

# Governance Performance Objectives

Governance performance reporting supports the following objectives:

- evaluate the effectiveness of the AI governance operating model;
- provide executive visibility into governance health;
- support evidence-based governance decisions;
- identify governance gaps and emerging issues;
- monitor implementation of governance activities;
- demonstrate organizational accountability; and
- drive continual improvement.

---

# Governance Reporting Principles

Governance reporting should be:

- evidence-based;
- timely;
- risk-informed;
- proportionate;
- objective;
- traceable;
- actionable; and
- understandable by its intended audience.

Reporting should enable decision-making rather than simply communicate information.

---

# Governance Reporting Levels

| Reporting Audience | Typical Reporting Focus |
|---|---|
| Executive Leadership | Overall governance health, strategic risks, governance maturity, major decisions, and significant issues. |
| AI Governance Committee | Governance implementation, cross-functional coordination, policy adherence, escalations, and outstanding actions. |
| Operational Management | Governance execution, operational issues, quality trends, monitoring outcomes, and improvement activities. |
| Risk Management | Risk exposure, treatment progress, residual-risk trends, and emerging governance risks. |
| Internal Audit | Governance evidence, assurance observations, remediation progress, and governance effectiveness. |

Reporting should be tailored to the responsibilities of each audience.

---

# Governance Performance Indicators

Governance Performance Indicators (GPIs) evaluate whether governance is operating effectively.

| Governance Area | Example Governance Performance Indicator |
|---|---|
| AI Intake | Percentage of AI initiatives entering governance before implementation. |
| AI Inventory | Percentage of governed AI systems recorded in the AI Inventory. |
| Impact Assessment | Percentage of required assessments completed before operational use. |
| Risk Management | Number of unresolved high-risk items and residual-risk acceptance trends. |
| Controls | Percentage of planned governance controls implemented and maintained. |
| Human Oversight | Coverage of required human-oversight activities. |
| Third-Party Governance | Percentage of AI providers reviewed in accordance with governance requirements. |
| Monitoring | Percentage of governed AI systems under active monitoring. |
| Assurance | Number of open assurance findings and remediation progress. |
| Change Governance | Percentage of material AI changes governed before implementation. |

These indicators assess governance capability rather than AI model quality or business performance.

# Governance Reporting

Typical governance reports may include:

- governance performance indicators;
- governance implementation status;
- governance maturity trends;
- significant governance decisions;
- material governance risks;
- governance exceptions;
- outstanding actions;
- assurance outcomes;
- monitoring summaries; and
- continual improvement initiatives.

Reporting frequency should be determined according to organizational governance needs and the significance of the information being reported.

---

# Management Review

Executive management should periodically review the AI governance program to determine whether it remains:

- effective;
- appropriately resourced;
- aligned with organizational objectives;
- responsive to changing business and regulatory conditions; and
- capable of supporting responsible AI adoption.

Management review should consider:

- governance performance indicators;
- governance reports;
- assurance results;
- significant incidents;
- unresolved governance issues;
- improvement initiatives; and
- recommendations for governance enhancement.

Management review should produce documented decisions and follow-up actions where appropriate.

---

# Continual Improvement

Governance reporting should support continual improvement by identifying opportunities to strengthen governance.

Improvement activities may arise from:

- monitoring;
- assurance;
- audit;
- incidents;
- governance reviews;
- stakeholder feedback;
- regulatory developments; and
- organizational learning.

Approved improvements should be tracked through established governance processes until completion.

---

# Governance Boundary

This document owns:

- governance performance objectives;
- governance reporting;
- governance performance indicators;
- management review;
- continual improvement reporting.

This document does not own:

- operational dashboards;
- AI model performance metrics;
- AI Inventory records;
- risk register entries;
- control testing results;
- assurance findings;
- incident records;
- change records.

Those matters belong to their respective governance capabilities.
