# Executive Summary

Enterprise AI governance requires clearly defined decision authority to ensure governance decisions are made consistently, transparently, and by the appropriate organizational functions.

This document establishes the governance decisions required throughout the lifecycle of the Megastar Intelligent Processor (MIP) and identifies the governance function responsible for making or approving each decision.

Clearly defined decision rights reduce ambiguity, strengthen accountability, support timely governance, and ensure that decisions are made by individuals with the appropriate business, technical, governance, and oversight responsibilities.

---

# Objective

The objective of this document is to define governance decision authority for MIP.

Decision rights establish who is authorized to approve governance decisions while ensuring that relevant stakeholders are consulted before significant decisions are finalized.

# Governance Decision Authority

| Governance Decision | Decision Authority |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Approve new AI use cases | Executive Sponsor |
| Approve significant business process changes supported by AI | Mortgage Operations |
| Approve significant AI platform enhancements | AI Platform Team |
| Approve AI governance policies, standards, and governance documentation | AI Governance Lead |
| Approve governance exceptions | AI Governance Lead |
| Approve production deployment of significant AI changes | AI Governance Lead, following consultation with Mortgage Operations, AI Platform Team, Risk Management, Privacy Office, Information Security, and Legal & Compliance |
| Accept residual AI risk following risk assessment | Mortgage Operations, informed by Risk Management |
| Approve privacy exceptions | Privacy Office |
| Approve security exceptions | Information Security |
| Interpret legal or regulatory obligations affecting AI governance | Legal & Compliance |
| Accept governance audit actions and management responses | Executive Sponsor |

# Decision-Making Principles

Governance decisions are guided by the following principles:

- Decision authority is assigned to the function with the appropriate business or governance accountability.
- Significant decisions are supported by relevant business, technical, governance, risk, privacy, security, and legal input where appropriate.
- Governance decisions are documented, traceable, and supported by appropriate evidence.
- Independent oversight is preserved throughout the governance process.
- Business ownership remains accountable for AI-enabled business outcomes.

---

# Escalation Principles

Where governance decisions cannot be resolved at the operational level, they are escalated through the governance structure.

Typical escalation scenarios include:

- Significant AI-related risks.
- Cross-functional disagreements.
- Governance policy exceptions.
- Material privacy or security concerns.
- Significant legal or regulatory implications.
- High-impact audit findings.

Escalation pathways are supported through the Governance Forums established within the Governance Operating Model.

---

# Decision Boundaries

This document establishes **decision authority only**.

It does not define:

- Operational responsibilities.
- Governance meeting structures.
- Governance review cadence.
- Governance workflows.
- AI risk treatment activities.
- Governance control implementation.

These subjects are addressed within their respective governance artifacts.

# Why This Document Matters

Clearly defined decision authority is essential for effective enterprise AI governance.

By establishing who is authorized to make governance decisions, Megastar Mortgage strengthens accountability, improves governance consistency, supports timely decision-making, and enables effective oversight throughout the lifecycle of MIP.

This document complements the Roles and Responsibilities artifact by defining **who has the authority to approve governance decisions**, rather than **who performs operational governance activities**.
