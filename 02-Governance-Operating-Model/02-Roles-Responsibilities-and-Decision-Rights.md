# Executive Summary

Enterprise AI governance succeeds when every stakeholder understands their role, responsibilities, and accountability.

Megastar Mortgage has established a cross-functional governance model in which business, technology, governance, and assurance functions work together to support the responsible operation of the Megastar Intelligent Processor (MIP).

This document defines the operational responsibilities assigned to each governance function. By clearly establishing ownership of governance activities, the organization promotes consistent execution, effective collaboration, and accountability throughout the AI lifecycle.

---

# Objective

The objective of this document is to define the operational responsibilities of each governance function supporting the implementation, operation, oversight, and continual improvement of MIP.

Clearly assigned responsibilities reduce ambiguity, strengthen collaboration, improve operational consistency, and enable effective enterprise AI governance.

---

# Governance Responsibilities

| Governance Function | Primary Responsibilities |
|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Executive Sponsor | Provide executive sponsorship, strategic direction, funding support, and organizational accountability for the AI governance program. |
| Mortgage Operations | Own AI-supported business processes, define operational requirements, monitor business outcomes, and ensure AI capabilities continue to support organizational objectives. |
| Mortgage Processing Team | Process mortgage documents using MIP, validate operational outputs during daily processing, report operational issues, and follow established governance procedures. |
| AI Governance Lead | Coordinate the AI governance program, maintain governance documentation, oversee governance implementation, facilitate governance activities, and report governance performance to management. |
| AI Platform Team | Operate, maintain, support, and continuously improve MIP while ensuring platform availability, enterprise integration, and operational stability. |
| Human-in-the-Loop Specialists | Validate AI-generated outputs, review low-confidence predictions, resolve processing exceptions, and verify business-critical information before operational use. |
| Quality Assurance Team | Perform quality reviews, validate processing accuracy, identify operational issues, support root cause analysis, and contribute to continuous process improvement. |
| Risk Management | Facilitate AI risk identification, assessment, monitoring, and enterprise risk oversight throughout the AI lifecycle. |
| Privacy Office | Ensure personal information is processed in accordance with organizational privacy requirements and applicable regulations. |
| Information Security | Protect AI platforms, enterprise infrastructure, user access, and organizational information assets through appropriate security governance. |
| Legal & Compliance | Interpret legal, regulatory, contractual, and internal policy requirements relevant to enterprise AI governance. |
| Internal Audit | Independently evaluate governance effectiveness, assess governance maturity, and provide assurance to executive management. |

# Shared Responsibilities

While responsibilities are assigned to individual governance functions, effective AI governance depends upon collaboration across the organization.

All governance stakeholders are expected to:

- Support responsible AI adoption.
- Maintain governance documentation relevant to their responsibilities.
- Participate in governance reviews and discussions.
- Escalate governance issues through established governance channels.
- Support governance assurance activities.
- Contribute to continuous governance improvement.

---

# Accountability Principles

Responsibilities within the Governance Operating Model are guided by the following principles:

- Accountability is clearly assigned.
- Responsibilities align with organizational expertise.
- Governance activities are performed collaboratively.
- Independent oversight is preserved.
- Business ownership remains accountable for business outcomes.
- Human judgment remains central to business-critical decisions.

---

# Responsibility Boundaries

This document establishes **operational responsibilities only**.

It does not define:

- Governance approval authority.
- Governance decision rights.
- Governance meeting structures.
- Governance performance reporting.
- Human oversight control procedures.
- AI risk treatment activities.

These subjects are addressed within their respective governance artifacts throughout the playbook.

---

# Why This Document Matters

Enterprise AI governance depends upon clearly defined operational ownership.

By assigning responsibilities across governance functions, Megastar Mortgage ensures that governance activities are performed consistently, accountability is maintained, and AI-assisted business processes operate within an effective governance framework.

This document establishes **who performs governance activities**, enabling subsequent artifacts to define **how governance decisions are made, governed, monitored, and assured**.
