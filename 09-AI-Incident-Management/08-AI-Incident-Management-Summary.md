# AI Incident Management Summary

## Executive Summary

The AI Incident Management Summary provides a consolidated view of AI incidents across Megastar Mortgage for a defined reporting period.

It draws from the Enterprise AI Incident Register and related authoritative governance records to show incident volume, severity, categories, response performance, root-cause themes, provider involvement, control weaknesses, corrective-action status, recurrence, escalation, and matters requiring governance attention.

The summary is designed for operational leadership, AI Governance, Governance Committees, and Executive Management.

It does not manage individual incidents, replace the Enterprise AI Incident Register, perform risk assessment, determine control effectiveness, approve corrective changes, accept residual risk, or close incidents.

Its purpose is to convert individual incident records into portfolio-level insight and governance action.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to establish a consistent approach for summarizing the performance and current condition of the AI Incident Management capability.

It enables Megastar Mortgage to:

- understand the current AI incident portfolio;
- identify changes in incident volume and severity;
- monitor response, containment, recovery, investigation, and closure performance;
- identify repeated and systemic incident patterns;
- assess corrective-action progress;
- identify recurring control, provider, data, human-oversight, monitoring, and change weaknesses;
- highlight unresolved High and Critical matters;
- identify resource, policy, control, and operating-model concerns;
- support Governance Committee and Executive review; and
- direct matters into Governance Oversight & Continual Improvement.

## Reporting Principles

Megastar Mortgage prepares AI Incident Management Summaries according to the following principles:

- Portfolio conclusions shall be supported by authoritative incident records.
- Open, closed, reopened, cancelled, and transferred incidents shall remain distinguishable.
- Severity shall reflect the current approved incident severity.
- Reported action completion shall remain distinct from verified completion.
- Incident closure shall remain distinct from unresolved matters transferred to another governance record.
- High and Critical incidents shall remain visible regardless of portfolio averages.
- Repeated and systemic patterns shall be reported separately from isolated events.
- Data limitations shall be disclosed.
- Incident trends shall be compared only where definitions and scope remain consistent.
- The summary shall identify required decisions and actions, not merely report counts.

## Summary Conclusions

The summary shall conclude the portfolio position using one of the following:

| Conclusion | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Stable | Incident activity is within manageable expectations and no material unresolved pattern requires elevated action. |
| Stable with Watch Items | Overall capability remains manageable, but emerging trends require closer monitoring. |
| Attention Required | Material incidents, delays, repeated themes, or action backlogs require coordinated governance response. |
| Escalation Required | Significant unresolved or systemic conditions exceed routine management authority. |
| Critical Intervention Required | Severe, uncontrolled, or potentially unacceptable conditions require immediate executive action. |
| Unable to Conclude | Incident data, evidence, or reporting quality is insufficient to support a reliable conclusion. |

This is a portfolio-management conclusion, not a risk-acceptance or management-review decision.
