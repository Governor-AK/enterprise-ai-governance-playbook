# AI Change Management Summary

## Executive Summary

The AI Change Management Summary provides a consolidated view of governed AI changes across Megastar Mortgage for a defined reporting period.

It draws from the Enterprise AI Change Register and supporting change records to show change volume, materiality, approval status, implementation outcomes, verification and validation results, emergency-change activity, post-implementation findings, failed or rolled-back changes, overdue actions, and matters requiring governance attention.

The summary is designed for operational leadership, AI Governance, Governance Committees, and Executive Management.

It does not manage individual changes, replace the Enterprise AI Change Register, approve changes, perform impact assessment, conduct verification, accept residual risk, or close specialist actions.

Its purpose is to convert individual change records into portfolio-level insight and governance action.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to establish a consistent approach for summarizing the current condition and performance of the AI Change Management capability.

It enables Megastar Mortgage to:

- understand the current AI change portfolio;
- identify changes in volume, materiality, and urgency;
- monitor approval and implementation performance;
- assess verification and validation outcomes;
- identify failed, rolled-back, deferred, or repeatedly delayed changes;
- monitor emergency-change activity and retrospective-review completion;
- identify recurring change-related incidents or control failures;
- assess post-implementation outcomes;
- identify overdue governance actions;
- support Governance Committee and Executive review; and
- direct material matters into Governance Oversight & Continual Improvement.

## Reporting Principles

Megastar Mortgage prepares AI Change Management Summaries according to the following principles:

- Portfolio conclusions shall be supported by authoritative change records.
- Registered, approved, implemented, verified, failed, rolled-back, cancelled, and closed changes shall remain distinguishable.
- Implementation completion shall remain distinct from successful verification and validation.
- Emergency changes shall remain visible until retrospective review is complete.
- Major and high-impact changes shall remain visible regardless of portfolio averages.
- Repeated and systemic weaknesses shall be reported separately from isolated change failures.
- Data limitations shall be disclosed.
- Trend comparisons shall use consistent definitions and scope.
- The summary shall identify required decisions and actions, not merely report counts.

## Overall Change Management Conclusion

The summary shall conclude the portfolio position using one of the following:

| Conclusion | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Stable | Change activity remains controlled and no material unresolved pattern requires elevated action. |
| Stable with Watch Items | Overall performance remains manageable, but emerging trends require closer monitoring. |
| Attention Required | Material delays, failures, deviations, or action backlogs require coordinated governance response. |
| Escalation Required | Significant unresolved or systemic conditions exceed routine operating authority. |
| Critical Intervention Required | Severe, uncontrolled, or potentially unacceptable change conditions require immediate executive action. |
| Unable to Conclude | Change data, evidence, or reporting quality is insufficient for a defensible conclusion. |

This is a portfolio-management conclusion, not a residual-risk or management-review decision.
