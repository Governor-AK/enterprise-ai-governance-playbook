# Framework Alignment

## Why This Capability Exists

The previous eleven capabilities define the Enterprise AI Governance Playbook.

Together, they establish the governance operating model used to govern AI systems throughout their lifecycle—from business context and AI inventory to risk management, controls, assurance, monitoring, incident response, change management, executive oversight, and continual improvement.

This capability serves a different purpose.

It does not introduce new governance processes, new documentation, new controls, or new operating requirements.

Instead, it demonstrates how the governance operating model established throughout this repository aligns with internationally recognized AI governance frameworks, standards, and regulations.

The objective is simple:

> Show where governance is operationalized—not recreate it.

---

## Scope

This capability provides alignment for:

- ISO/IEC 42001
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)
- European Union AI Act
- ISO/IEC 23894
- OECD AI Principles

It concludes with an enterprise crosswalk that maps the repository's governance capabilities against all supported frameworks.

---

## Alignment Philosophy

This repository was designed as an enterprise AI governance operating model.

It was not built around any single framework.

Each alignment document demonstrates how existing governance capabilities satisfy externally recognized expectations without changing the operating model itself.

Frameworks are treated as reference models.

The Enterprise AI Governance Playbook remains the authoritative governance system.

---

## Alignment Principles

Every alignment document follows the same principles.

- Existing governance capabilities remain the authoritative source.
- No new governance processes are introduced.
- No duplicate registers, policies, procedures, or templates are created.
- Operational responsibilities remain within Capabilities 01 through 11.
- Framework requirements are mapped to existing governance evidence.
- Alignment does not imply certification, accreditation, or legal compliance.
- One governance capability may support multiple framework requirements where appropriate.

---

## Repository Relationship

Capabilities **01–11** answer one question:

> **How does the organization govern AI?**

Capability **12** answers a different question:

> **How does that governance operating model align with internationally recognized frameworks?**

This distinction preserves a clear separation between governance implementation and framework interpretation.

## Documents

### 01 — ISO/IEC 42001 Alignment

Demonstrates how the Enterprise AI Governance Playbook aligns with the requirements of ISO/IEC 42001 by mapping governance capabilities to the AI management system requirements defined by the standard.

---

### 02 — NIST AI RMF Alignment

Demonstrates how the repository operationalizes the four core functions of the NIST AI Risk Management Framework—Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage—through existing governance capabilities.

---

### 03 — EU AI Act Alignment

Maps repository capabilities to governance obligations associated with the European Union AI Act, including risk management, documentation, human oversight, transparency, post-market monitoring, and governance accountability where applicable.

---

### 04 — ISO/IEC 23894 Alignment

Demonstrates how enterprise AI risk governance, assessment, treatment, monitoring, and oversight are addressed through the repository's existing risk management capabilities.

---

### 05 — OECD AI Principles Alignment

Shows how the repository supports internationally recognized AI governance principles, including accountability, transparency, robustness, human oversight, privacy, and responsible governance.

---

### 06 — Framework Crosswalk

Provides a consolidated cross-reference showing how each governance capability within the Enterprise AI Governance Playbook aligns across all supported frameworks.

The crosswalk serves as a single navigation point for readers seeking framework traceability.

## Conclusion

Frameworks describe what organizations should achieve.

This repository demonstrates how those expectations can be operationalized through a structured enterprise AI governance operating model.

Capability 12 does not extend the operating model.

It provides traceability between the governance capabilities already established throughout this repository and the external frameworks that organizations commonly use to evaluate AI governance maturity.
