# AI Control Posture Summary

## Executive Summary

The AI Control Design Standard establishes how governance controls are designed. The Enterprise AI Control Register maintains the authoritative record of approved controls. The AI Control Implementation Record confirms that approved controls have been implemented and supported by appropriate evidence.

The AI Control Posture Summary provides management with a consolidated enterprise view of the organization's AI control environment before independent AI Assurance begins.

Rather than duplicating individual control records, this document summarizes enterprise control coverage, implementation progress, outstanding governance gaps, implementation readiness, and overall preparedness for AI Assurance.

The summary enables governance leadership to determine whether the implemented control environment is sufficiently complete, governed, and documented to proceed to independent assurance.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide management with an enterprise-level view of the organization's AI control posture.

This report consolidates information from approved control records and implementation activities to:

- evaluate enterprise control coverage;
- summarize implementation progress;
- identify governance gaps;
- identify implementation dependencies;
- assess readiness for AI Assurance; and
- support governance decision-making.

The AI Control Posture Summary does not replace individual control records maintained within the Enterprise AI Control Register.

---

## Governance Approach

The AI Control Posture Summary is prepared after implementation activities have been completed.

Information is consolidated from:

- AI Control Design Standard;
- Enterprise AI Control Register; and
- AI Control Implementation Records.

The report summarizes the overall control environment without reproducing detailed control documentation.

---

## Enterprise Control Overview

The summary provides management visibility into:

| Enterprise Measure | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Approved Controls | Overall governance coverage |
| Implemented Controls | Current implementation status |
| Controls Pending Implementation | Outstanding implementation work |
| Controls Ready for Assurance | Assurance readiness |
| Controls with Outstanding Dependencies | Governance attention required |
| Controls with Outstanding Issues | Implementation gaps requiring action |

---

## Governance Coverage

Management evaluates whether:

- approved AI risks have appropriate control coverage;
- approved controls have been implemented;
- implementation evidence has been retained;
- governance dependencies have been addressed;
- material implementation gaps remain visible; and
- the organization is prepared for independent AI Assurance.

---

## Implementation Readiness

Implementation readiness considers:

- implementation completion;
- implementation evidence;
- implementation ownership;
- dependency resolution;
- outstanding implementation issues; and
- assurance readiness.

Readiness does not determine control effectiveness.

Control effectiveness is evaluated during AI Assurance.

---

## Governance Observations

The summary should identify:

- significant implementation themes;
- areas requiring management attention;
- recurring implementation issues;
- dependency concentrations;
- implementation risks requiring governance visibility; and
- matters affecting readiness for AI Assurance.

---

## Readiness Decision

Management determines whether the organization's implemented AI control environment is ready to progress into AI Assurance.

Possible readiness outcomes include:

| Decision | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Ready | AI Assurance may begin. |
| Conditionally Ready | Minor governance matters remain but do not prevent assurance. |
| Not Ready | Material implementation issues require resolution before assurance. |

## Why This Document Matters

Management requires more than individual implementation records to understand whether the organization's AI control environment is prepared for independent evaluation.

The AI Control Posture Summary consolidates implementation information into a single enterprise view, allowing governance leaders to assess overall readiness, identify remaining gaps, and determine whether AI Assurance should begin.

This document provides the governance bridge between implementation and independent assurance.
