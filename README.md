# Enterprise AI Governance Playbook

### From Principles to Practice

The Enterprise AI Governance Playbook is an open, practitioner-designed reference implementation for building and operating AI Governance within enterprise environments.

Rather than introducing another governance framework, this repository demonstrates how AI Governance can be operationalized through practical governance capabilities, enterprise operating models, reusable governance artifacts, implementation guidance, and structured decision-making across the AI lifecycle.

The objective is simple:

> **Turn governance principles into consistent enterprise practice.**

---

# Why This Playbook

Most AI Governance resources explain:

- what organizations should govern; and
- why governance matters.

Far fewer demonstrate how governance actually operates inside an enterprise.

Once an organization decides to govern AI, practical questions quickly emerge.

- How does an AI system enter governance?
- Where is it recorded?
- How are governance decisions documented?
- How are risks identified and managed?
- How are controls designed and verified?
- How are third-party AI providers governed?
- How are incidents and changes managed?
- How is governance monitored over time?
- How does governance continuously improve?

This repository focuses on answering those implementation questions through a practical governance operating model rather than theoretical discussion alone.

---

# Design Philosophy

The playbook is organized around **enterprise governance capabilities**.

Each capability owns a distinct governance responsibility, produces clearly defined outputs, and hands those outputs to the next governance activity through controlled processes.

Every artifact exists for a single governance decision.

Every enterprise record has a single authoritative owner.

Specialist capabilities contribute approved information rather than creating duplicate records.

This approach preserves:

- accountability;
- traceability;
- consistency;
- auditability; and
- proportional governance throughout the AI lifecycle.

# Governance Principles

The repository is built around several architectural principles that remain consistent across every capability.

- One governance decision belongs to one artifact.
- One authoritative enterprise record exists for every governed object.
- Governance capabilities enrich authoritative records through controlled governance processes rather than maintaining duplicate records.
- Dashboards, summaries, and reports consume approved governance information rather than redefining it.
- Every capability has clearly defined ownership boundaries.
- Governance decisions remain traceable throughout the AI lifecycle.
- Continuous improvement is driven by evidence rather than opinion.

# Framework Alignment

The implementation approach draws upon internationally recognized governance and risk-management practices, including:

- NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)
- ISO/IEC 42001 — Artificial Intelligence Management System
- ISO 31000 — Risk Management
- ISO/IEC 27001 — Information Security Management
- EU AI Act
- COBIT

These frameworks provide governance direction.

This repository demonstrates one practical approach for operationalizing those principles within an enterprise AI Governance program.
